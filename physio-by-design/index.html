Approach

Four principles that shape every session.

Less protocol, more attention. The result is a treatment plan you understand and can actually carry between visits.

i.

Assess before treating

Every new patient gets a full assessment first. We don't treat on the first visit unless it's appropriate — we figure out what's going on.

ii.

Hands-on, then home work

Manual therapy where it helps, exercise prescription that fits your week. The exercises are written down and rehearsed in-session so you'll actually do them.

iii.

One physio, every session

The same practitioner from intake to discharge. No re-explaining your story to a new face at every appointment.

iv.

Plain language

You'll leave each session knowing what we did, why we did it, and what's next. No mystery, no medical opacity.

About the practice

A small, deliberate physio practice.

Physio by Design sits at Unit 1, 89 Marine Parade — one room, by appointment, no waiting-area shuffle. The room was set up for the work, not the brand: a treatment bench, exercise space, plenty of natural light.

The practice is built around the idea that physiotherapy works better when it has time and continuity. So sessions run an hour where most run twenty. Same practitioner every visit. Plans written down, not improvised.

It's not faster physio. It's not cheaper physio. It's physio designed around the patient — and the patients who want that, find it.

If that's what you've been looking for, the door is open.

Sessions

What a first visit actually involves.

i.
Intake

Conversation about your history, what's bothering you, what you've tried, and what success looks like. Twenty minutes, no rush.

ii.
Assessment

Movement screen, range-of-motion testing, palpation of the relevant areas. We work out what's actually going on, not what we'd guess.

iii.
Plan & first treatment

Written plan: estimated session count, what each is for, what to do between. Then, if appropriate, we start treating that day.
